Steve Jobs

Technology and innovation visionary

Here's a time line of Steve Jobs

  • 1955: Steve Jobs is born on February 24th in San Francisco, California.
  • 1972: Attends Reed College but drops out after six months.
  • 1974: Works at Atari, a video game company, as a technician.
  • 1976: Co-founds Apple Computer, Inc. with Steve Wozniak in his parents' garage.
  • 1980: Apple releases the Apple III and later the Apple Lisa.
  • 1984: Introduces the Macintosh during an iconic commercial aired during the Super Bowl.
  • 1985: Is ousted from Apple after an internal power struggle with CEO John Sculley.
  • 1986: Co-founds NeXT Inc., a computer company.
  • 1986: Purchases Pixar Animation Studios from Lucasfilm.
  • 1997: Apple acquires NeXT Inc., bringing Steve Jobs back to the company.
  • 1998: Launches the iMac, a groundbreaking computer in terms of design and functionality.
  • 2001: Introduces the iPod, redefining the digital music industry.
  • 2003: The iTunes Store is launched, allowing for online music sales.
  • 2007: The iPhone is released, ushering in the era of modern smartphones.
  • 2010: Unveils the iPad, popularizing tablets.
  • 2011: Steve Jobs passes away on October 5th at the age of 56 after a battle with pancreatic cancer.
  • 2021: Apple continues to be one of the most influential and profitable
    technology companies in the world, leaving behind a lasting legacy from
    Steve Jobs.
